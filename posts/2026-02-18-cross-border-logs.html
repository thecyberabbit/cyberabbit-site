Regulators enforce cross-border logging limits

Feb 18, 2026 • 2 min read

New guidance forces teams to delete logs flowing between jurisdictions within 72 hours—or pay fines.

Data residency rules now treat logging pipelines as data exports. Teams must either keep logs local or explicitly delete them shortly after ingestion if they pass through different legal borders.

If your observability stack spans continents, automate the filtering and retention policies. A mirror metric in the EU is now considered an export unless the data is anonymized.

Audit your archived logging buckets for cross-region replication. If you cannot delete immediately, document the legal exception within your compliance runbooks.

Takeaway: Log shipping is not neutral. Treat it as a regulated stream and build the retention automation now.
