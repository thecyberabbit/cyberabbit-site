AI voice phishing clones CEO in real time

Feb 22, 2026 • 3 min read

Call center operators were fooled by a generative voice model reading the CEO’s phrases verbatim during a financial request.

The attacker recorded a public keynote, extracted the cadence, and used a fine-tuned voice model to fake a live call. The operator passed a wire transfer because the voice and phrasing sounded legitimate.

Defenders are now pairing verbal cues with video or secure tokens. If a voice request mentions a live keynote, pause and verify via an independent channel.

Also, track inbound voice requests for repeated phrases within the same minute—generative models often repeat unnatural filler words.

Takeaway: Voice no longer equals identity. Blend proofing and tokens when potential damage equals a bank transfer.
