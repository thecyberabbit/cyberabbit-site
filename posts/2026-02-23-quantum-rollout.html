Quantum-resistant keys land in production PKI

Feb 23, 2026 • 4 min read

After the NIST PQC standard, a global bank pushed hybrid keys across its PKI, and it’s the first production rollout worth noting.

The upgrade combines classical ECC certificates with CRYSTALS-Kyber wrappers. Customer-facing TLS terminated on load balancers that now require firmware updates for the new handshake.

Compatibility hiccups arose in backend services that still expected traditional RSA; a short-lived outage forced the bank to toggle back temporarily. Keep fallbacks ready when you roll out quantum-safe suites.

Key management is now layered. Rotate the Kyber seeds as often as the classical keys, monitor entropy pools, and watch for telemetry that still advertises old key sizes.

Takeaway: PQC isn’t just another patch. Treat it as a parallel stack: new encodings, new telemetry, and new audit requirements.
