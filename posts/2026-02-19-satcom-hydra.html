Hydra operators tamper with satellite uplinks

Feb 19, 2026 • 3 min read

Intercepted commands rerouted telemetry, and the same attackers probe the ground-station APIs for further privilege.

The attack chain started with phishing against satellite operations staff. Hydra quickly pivoted to the ground-station console, replayed valid commands, and forwarded telemetry to a shadow orbit.

Satellite vendors now mandate multi-factor for their uplink consoles and segmented networks for telemetry playback. They also added anomaly detection for repeated, low-latency command patterns.

If you manage any satcom gear, look for unexplained packet bursts toward the uplink, add checksum verification, and keep the vendor consoles off your general admin VLANs.

Takeaway: Space infrastructure is not immune. Treat satellite consoles like any other critical asset.
